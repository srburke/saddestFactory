About
Saddest Factory
Records

Saddest Factory Records is an American record label that was founded on October 5, 2020, by Phoebe Bridgers. The label operates as an imprint of Dead Oceans, promoting undiscovered artists across all genres and creative relationships between both record label and artist. Saddest Factory contracts typically cover three albums in a profit-split deal, with the label only having rights to recordings and taking no cut of publishing or live revenue. Artists that have been signed to the label include MUNA, Claud, Charlie Hickey, and Sloppy Jane (ordered left to right below).

Our Founder Phoebe Bridgers

For Phoebe Bridgers, Saddest Factory marks a new chapter in her career as one of indie's biggest stars. Since releasing her 2017 debut, Stranger in the Alps, she has steadly built word-of-mouth buzz thanks to her intimately detailed, quietly devastating songwriting, following her 2020 album Punisher, which deeply resonanted with national mood. With her own label, Bridgers offers artists a chance to similarly grow at their own pace, with little interference and all the resources of the Secretly Group team (Dead Oceans, Secretly Canadian and Jadjaguwar).
