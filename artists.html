MUNA

MUNA is an American indie pop band consisting of lead singer/songwriter Katie Gavin, lead guitarist Josette Maskin, and guitarist/producer Naomi McPherson. They released two studio albums with RCA Records, About U (2017) and Saves the World (2019), before signing to Saddest Factory Records, where their third studio album, MUNA (2022) was released.

Since MUNA began making music together in college, at USC, they'd always embraced pain as a bedrock of longing, a part of growing up, and an inherent factor of marginalized experience. The band's self-titled third album is an exquisite act of musical justice for those who suffered the indignities of growing up queer in the Y2K era.

CLAUD

In 2018, Claud, who is nonbinary and uses they/them pronouns, enrolled in Syracuse University's music and entertainment industry program; during their first week, they met Josh Mehling and Claire Cottril, who records as Clairo, and instantly hit it off, going on to create the EP titled Toast (2018) with the help of Mehling.

Garnering attention, they were invited to open for the Los Angeles-based duo The Marias, where they continued to hone their voice, polishing a sound that combines dreampop with late-'90s hooks. Nearly three years later at age 21, Claud, has released their first full-length album Super Monster (2021), debuting as Saddest Factory Records first signee and release.

Charlie Hickey

Having grown up in Pasadena, singer/songwriter Charlie Hickey moved away to college at the same time that he got more serious about music, and found himself moving back and forth between his hometown as his desire to play around with song ideas and demos with his friend and collaborator Marshall Vore took root.

Signing to Saddest Factory Records in 2021, the 22-year-old released his debut EP, Count the Stairs (2021), eminating the sound of soft-rock not far from regular collaborators Christian Lee Hutson and Harrison Whitford. Now with his latest release, debut full-length album, Nervous at Night (2022), Hickey forgoes gritty melancholy and instead lingers in the space between bedroom and pop as he navigates the travails of growing up.

Sloppy Jane

Haley Dahl, is the 24-year-old frontwoman, bandleader, and conductor of the Brooklyn-based 11-piece avant-rock act called Sloppy Jane. Dahl, originally from New York, spent most of her childhood and adolescence living in Los Angeles, meeting the likes of Phoebe Bridgers in high school, where they started the early days of Sloppy Jane as a three-piece punk band with Bridgers on Bass, Sarah Cath on Guitar, and Imogen Teasley-Vlautin on drums.

Signing to Saddest Factory Records in 2021, Dahl made her Saddest Factory debut with the release of the full-length album Madison (2021), an intimate record that is a grand gesture, a statement about big love, and about growing into yourself in the process.
